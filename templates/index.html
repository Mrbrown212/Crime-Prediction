Uk Crime Rate Prediction System

About Uk Crime Rate Prediction System

In order to better understand crime patterns and predict potential crime hotspots, law enforcement agencies are finding that crime rate prediction is an invaluable tool. Law enforcement organisations can more effectively direct resources to regions that are anticipated to see spikes in criminal activity by forecasting future crime trends. Both a general decline in crime and an improvement in public safety could result from this. Predicting crime rates can also assist law enforcement agencies in creating more effective plans for handling crimes as they occur.

The Police recorded crime and outcomes open data tables publication, which is accessible on the official UK government website, is the basis for the manual preparation of the dataset. Statistics on crimes committed in 19 cities between 2014 and 2021 are provided by this data. We can forecast the crime rates for three distinct categories of crimes that are anticipated to occur in the 19 cities over the course of the upcoming few years with the aid of this application. It contains data on ten distinct categories of criminal activity, such as criminal damage and arson, drug offences and fraud offences.

The Random Forest Regression model from scikit-learn, which accepts year, city name, and crime type data as inputs, is used by the system. One kind of ensemble learning technique that can be used to forecast continuous values from a set of data is Random Forest Regression. In order for it to function, a huge number of "decision trees" that each predict something about the target variable are created. After that, a final prediction is generated by averaging each prediction. Because of this, it is more accurate than a single decision tree. Using the testing dataset, the model predicts the crime rate with a 93.20% accuracy rate.
